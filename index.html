Research

I am broadly interested in robotic related topics (if you group such topics in Control, Planning/Learning, and Perception), as my academic background mixed mechanical, computer, and electrical engineering. That being said, my focus area is robotic perception, especially tactile sensing for enhancing robotic dexterity and control under contact-rich tasks. I believe multimode perception with tactile information input will help us build safer human-robot interactions.

Currently I'm working on developing a high-coverage, high-durability, and high-frequency tactile sensing skin for humanoid robotic hands, under the guidance of Prof. J. Edward Colgate at HAND ERC, Center for Robotics and Biosystems, Northwestern University.

My teammate is Neil Ghose.
This is the our final project for ECE470: Introduction to Robotics, Fall 2024

We worked on an UR3 robot drawing system using OpenCV, ROS, and inverse kinematics to plan optimal continuous paths by grouping edges and minimizing redundant motion in real-world coordinates.

The classic Inverse Pendulum
My teammates are Anay Koorapaty & Jai Anchalia.
This is the our final project for ECE486: Control Systems, Spring 2024

We modeled and linearized a reaction-wheel pendulum; designed PD and 2-/3-state full-state feedback with friction compensation and a decoupled observer; validated in Simulink and lab to balance upright, then added a switching controller to stabilize the bottom equilibrium.
